>[!Note]
Below is a list of notes for common battery conditions and defects found on devices with batteries
## Good Health
```
the battery's health has degraded by 10% or more, indicating Minor usage.
```
## Fair Health
```
the battery's health has degraded by 15% or more, indicating a diminishing capacity.
```
## Damaged Health
```
the battery health has degraded by 30% or more, indicating significant capacity loss and reduced runtime.
```
## Not Detected
```
The battery is not detected, indicating it is either missing or has reached the end of its usable life
```
## End of Life
```
The device’s battery has reached the end of its usable life and is no longer capable of retaining a charge, requiring replacement to restore normal operation.
```
## Driver Complication
```
Battery is not detected due to a driver complication
```
## Bloating
```
The device’s battery has begun to swell, indicating an internal fault and presenting a potential safety hazard. The battery should be replaced immediately to prevent further damage or risk.
```
