> **Purpose**:
> Standardized process for testing smartwatches to ensure consistent evaluation of functionality, condition, and resale or recycling eligibility.

> **Scope**:
> Applies to all smartwatches received for inspection and testing, including but not limited to Apple Watch, Samsung Galaxy Watch, Google Pixel Watch, and other wearable devices.

> **Audience**  
> QA technicians, warehouse staff, refurb/testing teams.

> [!note]
> This procedure is intended for **post-receipt testing** and does not cover customer-facing intake steps.

## 🧰 Required Tools & Setup

- Compatible charging cables / docks
- iPhone / Android test devices
- Stable Wi‑Fi network
- Bluetooth enabled
- Testing software or internal diagnostics tools
- Cleaning cloth

> [!tip]
> Always test devices on a **known-good phone** with no active accounts.

## 📥 Device Intake Check

- Scan Device ID into System Admin Page
- Associate Device
- Verify model & brand
- Copy [[SmartWatch Details]] into note section for documentation as you proceed with testing

> [!warning]  
> Do not power on devices showing signs of battery swelling or severe liquid damage.

## 🔋 Power & Charging Test

- Place the watch on the charger.
- Confirm charging icon appears.
- Allow device to power on.
- Verify battery percentage increases. (minimum 5% in 10 minutes)
- Let device charge to at least 20% for sufficient amount of power for testing.

> [!failure]
> If the device does **not power on**, mark as *Power Failure*

## 📲 Pairing & Setup

- Factory reset the smartwatch. (if necessary)
- Pair with the designated test phone.
- Confirm successful Bluetooth connection. (if applicable)
- Complete initial setup screens. (if applicable)

> [!important]
> Ensure **Activation Lock / Google Lock** is fully removed.

## 🔎 Physical Inspection

-  Record model, size, color, and serial number
-  Inspect display for cracks, burn-in, or dead pixels
-  Inspect casing for scratches, dents, or separation
-  Inspect Side Button for tactile feedback
-  Inspect rotating bezel (if applicable)
-  Inspect band connection and locking mechanism

> [!warning]
> **Severe physical damage** may qualify the device for recycling or damaged evaluation.

## 🛠️ Functional Testing Checklist

Test and confirm the following:

- Touchscreen responsiveness
- Physical buttons / crown
- Speakers and microphone
- Vibration / haptics
- Wi‑Fi connectivity
- Bluetooth stability
- Sensors (heart rate, motion, etc.)

> [!note]
> Not all models support the same sensors—test only what applies.

## 📶 Connectivity & Network

- Connect to Wi‑Fi.
- Sync data with paired phone.
- Test notifications (call, text, app alerts).

> [!tip]
> LTE models do **not** require SIM activation for basic functionality testing.

## 🔄 Factory Reset (Post‑Test)

- Unpair from test phone.
- Perform a full factory reset.
- Confirm setup screen appears after reset.

> [!success]
> Device is now cleared and ready for fulfillment or next processing step.

## 📝 Documentation

- Record test results in internal system.
- Assign final condition grade.

>[!info]
> Utilize standard [[Device Conditions#Smartwatches|Device Conditions]] for grading
