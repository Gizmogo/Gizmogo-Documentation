> **Purpose**:
> To establish a standardized process for testing cameras and lenses to ensure functionality, optical performance, cosmetic condition, and resale readiness.

> **Scope**:
> Applies to all DSLR, mirrorless, compact cameras, cinema cameras, and interchangeable lenses received for evaluation, resale, or refurbishment.

> **Audience**:
> QA technicians, warehouse staff, refurb/testing teams.

## 🧰 Tools & Equipment

### Camera Testing Tools

- Fully charged compatible battery
- AC adapter (if available)
- Formatted memory card
- Test chart (ISO 12233 recommended)
- Color checker card
- External monitor (optional)
- Sensor cleaning kit

### Lens Testing Tools

- Compatible camera body
- Focus chart (45-degree focus test sheet)
- Bright continuous light source
- Flash unit (optional)
- Blower and microfiber cloth

> [!caution]
> - Always power off equipment before mounting or removing lenses.
> - Avoid touching sensor or internal mirror components.
> - Use anti-static precautions when necessary.
> - Handle lenses by the barrel, not the focus or zoom rings during mounting.

## 📥 Receiving Device

- Scan Device ID into System Admin Page
- Associate Device
- Verify model & brand
- Inspect packaging contents

> [!warning]  
> Do not power on devices showing signs of battery swelling or severe liquid damage.

## 🔋 Power & Charging Test

- Insert battery and power on
- Navigate full menu system

> [!failure]
> If the device does **not power on**, mark as *Power Failure*

## 🔎 Physical Inspection

### Camera body

- Record model, size, color, and serial number
- Body integrity (cracks, dents, missing screws)
- LCD screen condition (dead pixels, scratches)
- Viewfinder clarity
- Port integrity (HDMI, USB, mic, etc.)
- Battery compartment condition

> [!warning]
> **Severe physical damage** may qualify the device for recycling or damaged evaluation.

### Lens

- Front and rear element scratches
- Fungus or haze
- Internal dust
- Oil on aperture blades
- Mount condition
- Filter thread damage

> [!tip]
> Inspect carefully under bright light.

## 🛠️ Functional Testing Checklist

### Sensor Test

> [!important]
> Perform at ISO 100–400 and also at high ISO (e.g., 3200+).

1. Mount standard test lens
2. Shoot blank white surface at f/16
3. Inspect for:
   - Dust spots
   - Hot pixels
   - Banding
1. Capture long exposure (10–30 seconds) to check for stuck pixels

### Mechanical Test

- Rotate focus ring (smoothness, resistance)
- Rotate zoom ring (if applicable)
- Test aperture ring (if manual)
- Listen for abnormal noises

> [!warning]
> Grinding sounds may indicate internal damage.

### Aperture Test & Sharpness Test

- Stop down through full aperture range
- Confirm blades close symmetrically
- Check exposure consistency

### Autofocus Test

> [!important]
> Perform at widest aperture first.

- Use focus chart at 45° angle
- Test center and edge AF points
- Confirm front/back focus accuracy
- Shoot at:
   - Widest aperture
   - f/5.6
   - f/8
- Evaluate center vs corner sharpness
- Test face/eye detection (if applicable)

> [!check]
> Test both single (AF-S) and continuous (AF-C) modes.

### Image Quality Test

- Mount camera on tripod
- Photograph ISO test chart
- Shoot at:
  - Base ISO
  - Mid ISO
  - High ISO
- Review sharpness, color accuracy, dynamic range

> [!note]
> Compare results to known-good reference body if possible.

### Video Test (If Applicable)

> [!info]
> Skip if model does not support video recording.

- Record at maximum resolution
- Check autofocus during recording
- Monitor overheating
- Verify audio input
- Inspect rolling shutter artifacts

### Stabilization Test (If Applicable)

> [!tip]
> Test handheld at slow shutter speeds.

- Enable stabilization
- Shoot at 1/10–1/30 sec
- Compare with stabilization off

## 🔄 Factory Reset (Post‑Test)

- Perform a full factory reset.
- Confirm setup screen appears after reset.

## 📝 Documentation

- Record test results in internal system.
- Log failures with clear notes.
- Record shutter count (if accessible)
- Assign final condition grade.
