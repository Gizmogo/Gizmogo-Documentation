## Speakers, Smart Speakers, and Audio Devices

### 1. Purpose
 >[!note]
This SOP defines the standardized process for receiving, inspecting, testing, grading, and processing speakers, smart speakers, and related audio devices to ensure consistent quality control, accurate valuation, and efficient handling.

### 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all wired and wireless speakers, smart speakers, soundbars, portable speakers, and similar audio devices processed by the operations team.

Examples include (but are not limited to):

- Bluetooth speakers
    
- Wi‑Fi smart speakers
    
- Home assistant speakers
    
- Soundbars
    
- Portable speakers

### 3. Responsibilities

- **Receiving Team**: Intake, labeling, and initial condition check
    
- **Testing Team**: Functional testing and diagnostics
    
- **Quality Control (QC)**: Final grading and issue verification
    
- **Inventory / Ops**: System updates and routing (resale, refurbish, recycle)

### 4. Intake & Receiving

1. Verify shipment matches the customer order.
    
2. Assign and attach internal tracking ID.
    
3. Photograph the device from all sides.
    
4. Record visible condition issues (scratches, dents, cracks, missing parts).
    
5. Check for included accessories:
    
    - Power cable / adapter
        
    - Remote (if applicable)
        
    - Original packaging (if present)

### 5. Initial Visual Inspection

Inspect for the following:

- Physical damage (cracks, dents, broken grills)
    
- Liquid exposure indicators (corrosion, residue, odor)
    
- Missing or damaged ports
    
- Signs of tampering or forced opening
    

If severe damage is found, mark as **Evaluation Required (ER)** and skip advanced testing.

### 6. Power & Boot Test

1. Connect the device to a verified power source.
    
2. Confirm the device powers on.
    
3. Observe LED indicators or display behavior.
    
4. Note boot errors, blinking error codes, or failure to power on.
    

Failure at this stage should be documented and flagged.

### 7. Functional Testing

#### 7.1 Audio Output Test

- Play test audio at low, medium, and high volume.
    
- Check for:
    
    - Distortion
        
    - Static or crackling
        
    - Unbalanced left/right channels
        
    - Speaker rattle
    
#### 7.2 Connectivity Test

- **Bluetooth**: Pair with test device and stream audio.
    
- **Wi‑Fi / Smart Features** (if applicable):
    
    - Connect to test network
        
    - Confirm app detection
        
    - Test voice assistant wake word
    
#### 7.3 Controls & Inputs

- Test all physical buttons
    
- Test touch controls (if applicable)
    
- Verify microphone functionality for smart speakers
    
- Test auxiliary / HDMI / optical inputs if present

### 8. Factory Reset & Account Check

1. Perform factory reset per manufacturer instructions.
    
2. Confirm device is not locked to a user account.
    
3. If account lock cannot be removed, mark as **Account Locked**.

### 9. Grading Standards

- **Grade A**: Fully functional, minimal cosmetic wear
    
- **Grade B**: Fully functional, moderate cosmetic wear
    
- **Grade C**: Functional with noticeable wear or minor issues
    
- **As‑Is / ER**: Non‑functional, damaged, or account locked
    

All grades must be supported with notes and photos.

### 10. Data Logging & System Update

- Update testing results in internal system
    
- Attach photos and tester notes
    
- Confirm final grade and disposition
- 
### 11. Disposition

Based on grading:

- **Resale**: Grade A / B
    
- **Refurbishment**: Grade C
    
- **Recycle**: Non‑functional or uneconomical repairs

### 12. Safety & Handling Notes

- Do not open sealed battery units unless authorized
    
- Handle lithium‑powered devices with care
    
- Follow recycling guidelines for damaged batteries
